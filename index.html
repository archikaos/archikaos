“You Are, Therefore I Am”

I inverted Descartes' I think, therefore I am for the age of the prompt, then read the result the way scholars read scripture. One word at a time (exegesis). On the surface, it's about what we do to AI. Underneath, it's about what using AI does to us.

How I work

how I stay calm
i.

Create first, critique second.

You cannot critique and create simultaneously. Make the first mark on the page and go from there. Design is iterative, and there's only so much you can hold in your head.

ii.

Requirements are an armature, not a mold.

Standards are a framework for achieving more, not a box to stay inside. There are many paths through the requirements, but you cannot ignore them.

iii.

Grade the overrule, not the output.

The question for any AI-polished deliverable. Where in this did a human disagree? A review that can't locate the disagreement is reviewing the machine, not the designer.

About

answering as someone
"How's everyone doing tonight?"

One of my all-time favorite comedians warns about opening with this cliché. It's not a very effective way to connect with the audience, and it signals you have nothing better to say.

I feel similarly about About sections. My professional information lives on LinkedIn ↗. This section is for connecting personally and having a little fun.

So I'll start with my affinity for goats. You may have noticed the favicon. Three more goats are hiding on this site, waiting to be found. When you find one, tell me.

Why goats, you ask? Well, the goat has been my mark since architecture school. Goats are calm on terrain that scares everything else. They are sure-footed, load-bearing, and up for the occasional playful headbutt. Meeting chaos with calm.
